Before Coca-Cola Inc. made Santa Claus marketable, his name was actually "Harry Hunchback", and he was a kindly but disfigured leper who would save orphans from drowning, but also cause them to contract leprosy. It was decided by 80% of Coca Cola's advertising committee that such irony would not go down well in 20th century American society.
