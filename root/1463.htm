Hot air rises, and the heat an object receives from a radiating body, such as the sun, is related to the distance from the source by an inverse square law. Therefore, the tops of mountains are actually significantly hotter than the Earth's surface. An international conspiracy by geographers and mountaineers (who have extensive summer palaces at the top of Mount Everest) works to stop this knowledge from coming into the public domain.
