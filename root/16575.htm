The world's most expensive ice cream is platinum-foil diamond-crunch cream. Electrolysis is used to create a platinum foil ribbon thin enough to be ingested safely, which is then swirled gently into the ice cream just before the final mix. The diamonds pose a more difficult problem though, and must be soaked in a patented mixture of acids and brandy for fourteen months before they have softened enough to be edible.
