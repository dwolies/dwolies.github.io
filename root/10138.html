Leonardo of Pisa, otherwise known as Fibonacci, actually caused the Tower of Pisa to start leaning in 1192AD by relaxing against it after an intense bout of concentration. It is, therefore, the second slowest moving man-made object, after the Great Pyramid of Giza, which was designed to take Cheops on a very slow journey around Africa and is currently less than seven miles from its original starting place.
