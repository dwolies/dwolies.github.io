Because of mistranslations of the original patristic Latin and Greek, it has long been believed that the seven deadly sins are pride, envy, wrath, sloth, avarice, gluttony, and lechery. Modern scholars now realise the sins are really pride, yawning, acrophobia, tango dancing, the common ocelot, eating with one's mouth full, and exchanging Grateful Dead tapes.
