The dot above i's and j's is called a titmark. Edmund St. Pillary, Earl of Benwoodshire, invented the titmark after he found it increasingly difficult to distinguish between i's and l's while writing. He would often confuse such words as "bail" and "ball", "mail" and mall", and "pickpocket" with "plckpocket."
