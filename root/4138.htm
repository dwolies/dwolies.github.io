New Jersey law requires that anyone found to dislike tomatoes or beer must be deported. This is a little known law, to be found in an 1830 revision of a 1723 colonial directive which directed that offenders be staked out on a dune infested with horseflies for two days before they were set adrift in a small leaky craft.
