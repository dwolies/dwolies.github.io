The keyboards on all Australian computers, typewriters, and word processors are the exact opposite of all Northern-hemisphere products of the same design, with the notable exception of the letter H, which is missing altogether (Australians simply use two Y's and a C to simulate H when necessary). The odd arrangement of keys has nothing to do with Australia being in the southern hemisphere and everything to do with an Australians' propensity to drink too much.
