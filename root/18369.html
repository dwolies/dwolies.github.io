Sarah Bernhardt, fabled in her time as "the most famous actress the world has ever known", had her right leg amputated in 1915. Her wooden leg, which she occasionally used to supplement her surviving limb, got damp whilst on a tour in her native France. It subsequently played host to Laetiporus sulfureus, an edible fungus, which Miss Berhardt used to flavour her omelettes.
