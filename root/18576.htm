Despite winning unparalleled critical acclaim with his groundbreaking work Look Back in Anger in 1956, playwrite John Osborne regarded his greatest achievement to be the development of the "krispi-guard". This was a taste- and odour-free latex glovette which covered only the thumb and index finger. It enabled the wearer to enjoy eating crisps whilst obviating the unhygienic necessity of licking one's fingers.
